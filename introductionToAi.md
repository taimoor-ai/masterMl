**Artificial Intelligence (AI)** is a field of computer science that focuses on creating machines or software that can perform tasks which normally require  **human intelligence** .

These tasks include:

* Learning from data
* Understanding language
* Recognizing images
* Making decisions
* Solving problems

AI systems try to  **simulate how humans think, learn, and act** .

---

# 1. Simple Definition

**Artificial Intelligence** means:

> Teaching computers to think, learn, and make decisions like humans.

Example:

* When a phone recognizes your face → AI
* When a website recommends products → AI
* When a chatbot answers questions → AI

# 4. Types of Artificial Intelligence

## 1. Narrow AI (Weak AI)

This AI performs  **only one specific task** .

Examples:

* Chatbots
* Image recognition
* Voice assistants

Example systems:

* ChatGPT
* Google Translate

Most AI today is  **Narrow AI** .

---

## 2. General AI (Strong AI)

This AI would think like a  **human brain** .

It could:

* learn anything
* reason
* solve new problems

Example:
A robot that can learn **medicine, programming, and driving** like a human.

This  **does not exist yet** .

---

## 3. Super AI

This would be  **smarter than humans** .

It could:

* invent new technologies
* solve world problems

Currently  **only theoretical** .

---

# 5. Core Components of AI

AI works using several technologies.

### 1 Machine Learning

A technique where computers  **learn from data instead of being explicitly programmed** .

Example:
Spam email detection.

Field: Machine Learning

---

### 2 Deep Learning

Uses  **neural networks similar to the human brain** .

Used for:

* image recognition
* speech recognition

Field: Deep Learning

---

### 3 Natural Language Processing (NLP)

Allows computers to  **understand human language** .

Example:
Chatbots and translators.

Field: Natural Language Processing

---

### 4 Computer Vision

Allows machines to  **see and understand images or videos** .

Example:
Face recognition systems.

Field: Computer Vision

---

# 6. How AI Works (Basic Workflow)

Step 1 — Data Collection
Large amounts of data are gathered.

Step 2 — Training
Algorithms learn patterns from data.

Step 3 — Model Creation
A trained model is created.

Step 4 — Prediction
The AI system makes decisions or predictions.

Example:
Email spam filter learns from thousands of emails.
